About

I am an iOS developer living in the greater New York City area. For the past three years, I have focused my efforts on learning Swift and using Xcode to build great mobile applications and games. Throughout the development of my applications, I have used libraries such as UIKit, SpriteKit, and SceneKit.

These technologies have allowed me to create a wide array of applications that include games, productivity applications, and social networks. Some of my hobbies include playing video games, graphic design, and Rubik's Cube speedsolving.

Gummy Gap

Gummy Gap is a game in which the player must maneuver their gummy between a series of pipes to avoid getting hit and have their Gummy squished. Every time the playerâ€™s Gummy makes it past one of the pipes, the player is awarded one point. During the stages between pipes, the player has the option to collect randomly generated coins which can be used to purchase new Gummies in the shop. In-app purchases offer opportunities to buy more coins and remove ads. The game supports Game Center so that players can compete against their friends to see who can obtain the highest score.

Gummy Gap was developed in a span of a month. It was first started in late February of 2016 and released on March 25, 2016. All code was written in Xcode 7 using Swift 2.0. SpriteKit was the core library used to render all graphics and all of the assets were designed using GIMP.

Mining Madness

In Mining Madness, the player must move their minecart to avoid the falling rocks. The cave is collapsing and all the precious gold can not be lost! Using this gold, the player has the option to purchase different colored rocks to customize their game. In-app purchases offer opportunities to buy more coins and remove ads. The game supports Game Center so that players can compete against their friends to see who can achieve the highest score.

Mining Madness was developed in a span of a few weeks. It was started in early April and was released on June 2, 2016. All code was written in Xcode 7 using Swift 2.0. SpriteKit was used as the core framework for all the graphics, physics, and collisions. All sprites were designed in GIMP.

Rong

Rong is my newest mobile game. It has been in development for about two weeks being started in early July. I hope to release the game sometime in August, putting the development period at about one month. The game will feature in-app purchases, advertisements, and customization.

Rong is being developed with SpriteKit. Unlike my other SpriteKit games, this one will feature audio and sound effects that are powered by AVFoundation and SpriteKit's audio engine. Stay tuned for more features and gameplay. All assets displayed are not officially subject to be in the final verion of the game.

Detour

Detour is a voxel-styled arcade racing game where accuracy is the key to winning. Stay on the road for as long as possible and watch out for any obstacles that will cause you to take a detour. Detour features five unique environments that the player can interact with in addition to over 10 different vehicles. Players can donate using in-app purchases to remove advertisements.

Detour was developed over the course of one month for a competition with the theme of cars. The competition was hosted by the iOS developer and YouTuber Jared Davidson who currently has over 60,000 subscribers. Detour was the winner of the contest and was officially released on October 31, 2016. Detour was developed using SceneKit to render the 3D models, SpriteKit to render the user interface, and AVFoundation to play sound effects and audio files. All voxel models were created in MagicaVoxel and all user interface components were designed using GIMP.
